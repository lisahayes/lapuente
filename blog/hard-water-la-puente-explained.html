Hard Water in La Puente: What 10 to 17 Grains Actually Means

Hardness is the most consequential number nobody reads on a water report. La Puente’s basin supply runs roughly 10 to 17 grains per gallon, firmly in the hard-to-very-hard range, and that single fact schedules half the plumbing maintenance in this city. The number, translated

A grain per gallon is a unit of dissolved calcium and magnesium, and the valley’s range works out to roughly 170 to 290 mg/L of mineral riding in every glass, shower, and wash cycle. The minerals are harmless to drink, hardness is a plumbing problem, not a health one, but they precipitate out wherever water heats, evaporates, or sits, and precipitation is the whole story: scale on fixtures, sediment in tanks, and crust at any joint that has ever seeped.

The source is geology, not treatment: basin water filters through mineral-rich alluvium, and hardness survives the safety treatment that handles everything else.

What each part of your house experiences

Water heaters take the heaviest tax: heat drives minerals out of solution fastest, so sediment blankets tank bottoms, insulates burners into early fatigue, and consumes anode rods ahead of the manual’s schedule, the full story of our heater page. Fixtures wear second: scale roughens the surfaces cartridges glide across, and the stiff-then-drippy faucet lifecycle runs years early here. And aging copper takes the long-term charge, since scale boundaries seed the pitting that becomes the valley’s pinhole signature.

None of this is dramatic in any given month. All of it compounds, which is why hard-water cities reward maintenance calendars over reaction.

Free and worth it: unscrew your faucet aerators twice a year and rinse the grit. In this water it is the highest-value minute of fixture maintenance available, and it solves most weak-flow complaints on the spot.

Softeners, honestly assessed

A softener exchanges the hardness minerals out and genuinely helps everything downstream of it: fixtures stay smooth, heaters sediment slower, and new pipe lives easier. What it cannot do is refund the past, wall thickness pitting has consumed stays consumed, and softened water is a comfort and longevity decision, not a rescue for sixty-year copper. Sizing and salt logistics deserve real numbers from your actual hardness, which we measure rather than assume.

For households weighing one alongside a repipe, the honest pairing is softener-protects-the-new-pipe, and the order of operations matters less than doing both against evidence.

Live well with hard water

The valley is not going to soften its geology, so the winning play is a calendar: aerators seasonally, heater annually, meter monthly, and evidence before any big spend. The pages below carry each habit’s deep version, and the phone carries the shortcuts: (626) 898-6169.
