The two arguments every east-side summer produces

Our pool drops close to an inch a day in July. Everyone says heat. Are they right?

They might be, which is why the bucket test exists: a weighted bucket on a step, both levels marked, twenty-four hours with the pump running, then compared. The east side’s hot-summer Mediterranean climate genuinely evaporates a lot of water, but it evaporates the bucket at the same rate as the pool. A pool that outdrops its bucket has a leak, whatever the thermometer says, and the testing sequence starts from that fact.

The lawn stays wet in a dry month but the sprinklers pass every visual check. What gives?

Visual checks watch the heads; leaks live in the laterals and valves between them. A zone can spray beautifully while bleeding underground, and the tell is exactly what you describe: wet ground keeping the controller’s schedule or, worse, ignoring it. Zone-by-zone isolation against the meter assigns the loss in an hour, and the repair is a keyhole at the located point rather than an excavated hunch.

Why the east side owns the outdoor-water workload

Pools cluster thickest on these streets, and the plumbing serving them was mostly buried decades ago in rigid PVC under decks and lawns that have been settling ever since. Add mature irrigation systems extended in stages toward the Covina line, and the east side’s outdoor water infrastructure outweighs its indoor plumbing for sheer buried footage. The workload follows: pool leak detection and its inground structural cousin lead our east-side dispatch, with irrigation system work close behind.

All of it drains the same metered basin water the kitchen uses, which keeps the economics simple: outdoor leaks here pay for their own repairs in saved billing, usually quickly.

East-side diagnostic gift: pump-correlated loss points at pressure-side pool plumbing, pump-off loss at shell and fittings. Telling us which pattern you see starts the visit a step ahead.

Heat, decks, and the seasonal leak

Inland heat does more than evaporate; it expands. Pool decks and slabs grow through a 90-degree afternoon and shrink overnight, working the skimmer throats and fittings bonded between structures, which is why some east-side leaks are seasonal performers: tight all winter, weeping all summer. We test with that physics in mind, running detection under the conditions that make shy failures speak, and reading a summer-only loss as the structural clue it is.

The Industry border’s working days fill these streets with schedules that do not bend for service calls, so evening and weekend testing windows are normal bookings here, not favors: (626) 898-6169 arranges either.

One more east-side habit pays off every summer: log your pool level against a fixed tile line for a week each June. That baseline turns any later argument about loss rates into arithmetic, and arithmetic is the cheapest diagnostic instrument a homeowner owns.
