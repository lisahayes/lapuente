The arithmetic under the verdict

Evaporation acts equally on pool and bucket, so subtracting the bucket’s drop from the pool’s isolates the unexplained loss, and unexplained inches convert to gallons through your surface area: square footage times inches over twelve, times 7.48 gallons per cubic foot, with a modest shape factor since few backyard pools are true rectangles. The thresholds are deliberately honest, a sixteenth of an inch is inside pencil-mark noise, while a fifth of an inch daily on a typical La Puente pool is hundreds of gallons a week that the meter is buying.

Inland heat is real here, summer evaporation alone can pull a quarter inch on a dry, hot day, which is exactly why the bucket exists: it measures the weather so you do not have to guess it.
